CPR & Yellow card

What is CPR number

Central Person Register (CPR) is the identification number that consists of 10 digits - first 6 stand for the date of your birth, the other 4 are assigned identification numbers.

You need a CPR number to access many services, e.g. receiving salary, transfering money, visiting the doctor or even using a library.

How to get it

In order to get a CPR number you first need to have a residence certificate and a place to live in Denmark.

Step 1

GATHER DOCUMENTATION

  • residence certificate
  • rental contract or other proof of your address (if you don’t have rental contract, you can download housing confirmation here)
  • confirmation of your enrollment to an education program
  • passport or personal ID

    • Step 2

    MAKE APPOINTMENT WITH CITIZEN OFFICE

    After you collect all the documents, you need to set a meeting at the citizen office. If you live in Greater Copenhagen, you also need to apply online here.

    To ensure that you are indeed staying in Denmark, you might be required to come in person to your local citizen service center or to an International Citizen Service center.

    During the application process you will choose your GP (general practitioner) - if you don’t have any specific requests, you will be assigned the one closest to your address.

    Step 3

    WAITING

    Within 30 days you should receive a yellow card to your address.

    You can read more about application in here.

    Help

    Yellow card is a card with your personal information (such as name, address,CPR number) and contact to your general practitioner. It entitles you to medical care, so you should keep it with you at all times, especially when you go to the doctor or a dentist.

    It will be sent to your address by post automatically, after applying for a CPR number. If you don’t receive it after 30 days from your registration you can call or visit your local citizen service center.
