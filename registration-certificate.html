Registration certificate

What is registration certificate

EU/EEA or Swiss citizens don’t have to apply for a visa. However, if you are staying in Denmark for longer than 90 days, you need to apply for a registration certificate. For students from EU/EEA & Switzerland it is free of charge.

It proves that you have the right to stay in Denmark and allows you to access various services, e.g. legal work, medical care, housing. You also need it to get a CPR number and NemID.

How to get it

In order to be registered as a student, you need to be enrolled in an educational program, study actively (which means coming to classes and participating in the exams) and be able to support yourself financially.

Step 1

GATHER DOCUMENTATION

  • your passport and/or your national ID card
  • passport-size photo of you
  • completed OD1 application form (here is a link to the form)
  • document that confirms your enrollment in a study program (e.g. acceptance letter from your school)

    • Step 2

    MAKE APPOINTMENT WITH SIRI

    When you gather all the documentation you need to book an appointment with SIRI (Danish Agency for International Recruitment and Integration). Here is a website on which you can do this.

    You can find the SIRI branch that is closest to you here.

    You can read more about booking a SIRI appointment in here.

    Step 3

    WAITING

    After booking a meeting you can go to the chosen SIRI’s office to submit your documents. When the decision for your case will be made you will receive an answer. The whole procedure usually takes up to 30 days.

    Help

    If you need some guidance with the first steps you can contact ICS here.

    You can read more about the residence permit here.

    *If you have lost your residence permit, you need to apply for a new one.
