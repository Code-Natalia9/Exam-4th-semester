Working in Denmark

What to know before taking up a job

EU/EEA/Swiss citizens don’t need a work permit to be employed. Some students might be able to find a job in their field of study. However, most of them will likely find a job in restaurants, bars, shops or distributing newspapers. There are no limitations on how many hours international students can work. Students usually work 10 - 20 hours a week.

As for internships it differs from company to company. They can be part-time, full-time, paid or unpaid.

How to get it

Step 1

GATHER DOCUMENTATION

To apply for a job you are most likely going to need:

  • CV
  • cover letter / motivational letter
  • it is also a big benefit if you also have a LinkedIn account

    • Step 2

    INTERVIEW

    Some employers might also request a meeting for an interview.

    Step 3

    GET A CONTRACT

    Remember to get a contract from your employer, so you will work legally and be entitled to all benefits.

    Step 4

    TAX CARD

    Don’t forget to get a tax card as soon as you get a job. You can read more about it here.

    Where to look for job

    There are many different ways to find a student job. You can start from:

  • checking career center in your educational institution (if there is one)
  • asking your classmates if they know of any offers
  • looking on LinkedIn
  • going to the student job fairs organized by Copenhagen Capacity (you can read more about it here)
  • checking an official Danish website here
